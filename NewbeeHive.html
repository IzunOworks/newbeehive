# NewbeeHive **NewbeeHive** is an **experimental guideline** designed to make it easier for beginners to ask questions. It is not a service, a forum, or a community. It simply proposes a way to ask questions using platforms that already exist. --- ## What is NewbeeHive? NewbeeHive is a **lightweight rule set** for asking questions without fear of being ignored, nitpicked, or corrected in excessive detail. Instead of creating a new Q&A site or social network, NewbeeHive suggests **simpler ways to use existing platforms**. The goal is not to get perfect answers. The goal is to make **asking questions itself feel easier**. --- ## What NewbeeHive does NOT aim to be NewbeeHive does not aim to be: - A permanent knowledge base - A moderated community - A place to judge the quality of questions - A system that ranks people or answers In NewbeeHive, questions may be: - Incomplete - Vague - Incorrect - Temporary And that is acceptable. --- ## How to use NewbeeHive ### Step 1: Use any platform you like X (Twitter), forums, blogs, or anywhere else **where people can read your post**. NewbeeHive itself does not provide a place to ask questions. --- ### Step 2: Use a simple question template A NewbeeHive-style question only needs three things: - What you want to do - What you tried - What didn’t work (or what you don’t understand) Precise technical terms are **not required**. --- ### Step 3: Add the `#NewbeeHive` tag When you post a question or share your thoughts, add the **`#NewbeeHive`** hashtag. This tag acts as a **loose marker** to make NewbeeHive-style posts easier to find. --- ### Step 4: Search with `#NewbeeHive` When you want to find questions or ideas from others, search for **`#NewbeeHive`**. You may find: - People stuck in similar situations - Explanations from those at a similar level - Imperfect but useful thought processes --- ### Step 5: Do not expect permanence Answers may disappear. Links may break. That is fine. If something matters to you, **save it yourself**. --- ## Learning by teaching NewbeeHive is not only for people who ask questions. Answering questions is also considered **part of the learning process**. - You do not need to be an expert - You do not need to be correct Answers like these are welcome: - “I’m not completely sure, but this is how I understand it” - “I might be wrong, but this worked for me” - “I was stuck at the same point” Trying to explain your understanding in your own words can help deepen that understanding. --- ## Status NewbeeHive is an **experimental guideline**. It may change. It may remain small. You do not need permission to use it. If it is useful, use it. If not, feel free to ignore it.
